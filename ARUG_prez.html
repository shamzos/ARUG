Outliers in general

Outliers are extreme values that deviate from other observations on data.

Outliers are represented in two type:

  • Ones with real occurence like fraudulent transaction in finance, people with an extremly high usage of data in telecom industry, income of client in millions and so one.
  • Ones whome occured erroneously like client with 200 years old, failure detection of temprature that result high values.

Regardless, the presence of outliers needs to be understood and will require special treatment.

Outliers in times series

In some field where time is an important dimension the conventional outlier detection wont be applicable due to the impact of time so some methode are developped during past years to detect and deal with these outliers (AKA anomalies).

Some of these methodes are:
- STL + IQR.
- Twitter + GESD.
- Sliding window-based forecasting method.
- Exponential smoothing.

In our case will study outliers in google trend seach data for the word ‘Vote’ between 2004 and 2019 using GESD and STL in Anomalize package.

Dealing with outliers:

What we need to know?

After Detecting outliers replacing theme is not always a solution, always it depends on our use case and our needs.

But in case we need to replace outlier for better prediction and removing noise there are some methodes:

1- Interpolation.

2- Kalman.

both of methodes above exist in imputeTS.

Dealing with outliers:

Data from Step 3 is saved in test.rds file.

library(imputeTS)
readRDS('test.rds') %>%
  mutate(observed = (ifelse(anomaly == 'Yes', NA, observed) %>%
                       na_kalman() %>%
                       as.integer()
                     )
         ) %>%
  ggplot( aes(x=date,y=observed,col=anomaly)) + geom_point()

If we need to use Interpolation instead we have just to chnage na_kalman() to na_interpolation().
